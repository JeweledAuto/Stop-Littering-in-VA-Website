Getting Involved Empowers You

Getting involved in helping maintain and clean up your local community and spreading awareness about the harm littering causes with your fellow community members will help you take less of a passive role in the condition of your community. Actions like these with help empower you and your community members to make changes and act against the growing issues littering causes in your area. Helping encourage everyone in your local area to take charge and fight for a cleaner and safer community.

Getting Involved Saves your Local Habitat

Getting involved and taking action against littering in your community helps empower you to maintain your community's ecosystem and visual appeal and protects both local residents and wildlife. Keeping your local habitat free of litter will help prevent harmful and potentially deadly effects domestic pets and local wildlife can experience when consuming many of the toxic products littering leaves in the environment. In addition, getting involved in the cleanup process helps prevent harmful chemicals from litter leeching into the soil and water, causing harm and contamination in both local water sources and plants.

Getting Involved Helps your Community

Getting involved not only helps you personally but also benefits and protects the greater community. Taking small personal actions against littering and spreading information about the harms littering causes will help bolster a more significant movement within your community, encouraging others to participate and make changes in their own lives promoting large-scale change within your community. This compounding change helps turn small lifestyle changes into community and state-wide transformations that will help redefine and protect the residents, habitat, and wildlife for decades to come.
